## Scenario 1: Morning Dashboard Review (HR_ADMIN)
**Persona:** Rachel Cohen - HR_ADMIN  
**Goal:** Get daily overview of recruitment activities  
**Frequency:** Daily, first thing in morning  

### Context
Rachel arrives at the office at 8:30 AM. She needs to quickly understand what happened overnight and what requires her attention today.

### Steps
1. Rachel opens her laptop and navigates to the CV Sorting System
2. System automatically logs her in (remembered session)
3. Dashboard loads showing:
   - 12 new CVs uploaded yesterday
   - 3 job positions with candidates ranked
   - 1 report generated by David (recruiter)
   - Pipeline metrics: 45 active candidates, avg. 18 days to-hire
4. She clicks on "Audit Logs" widget
5. Reviews last 24 hours of activity:
   - David uploaded 8 CVs for "Senior Developer" position
   - Sarah (another recruiter) ranked candidates for "Product Manager"
   - No unusual activities detected
6. She notices time-to-hire increased by 2 days
7. Clicks through to detailed report to investigate
8. Identifies bottleneck in interview scheduling stage
9. Makes note to discuss with team in weekly meeting

### Expected Outcome
✅ Rachel has complete visibility into system activities  
✅ Identified trend requiring attention within 5 minutes  
✅ Confident all compliance requirements met  
✅ Ready for day with action items  

### System Requirements
- Persistent login with secure session
- Real-time dashboard updates
- Audit log with 24-hour filter
- Trend indicators (up/down arrows)
- Quick drill-down to details

## Scenario 2: Bulk CV Upload for New Position (HR_RECRUITER)
**Persona:** David Levi - HR_RECRUITER  
**Goal:** Upload and process 35 CVs for new Software Engineer position  
**Frequency:** 2-3 times per week  

### Context
David just received 35 CVs from LinkedIn and email for a new Software Engineer position. He needs to get them into the system quickly and start screening.

### Steps
1. David logs into the system
2. Navigates to "Jobs" section
3. Clicks "Create New Job"
4. Fills in job details:
   - Title: "Senior Software Engineer"
   - Required skills: Python, React, AWS, Docker
   - Experience: 5+ years
   - Location: Tel Aviv
5. Saves job (system assigns ID: JOB-2026-015)
6. Clicks "Upload CVs" button
7. Drag-and-drops all 35 PDF files into upload zone
8. System shows progress bar: "Uploading files... 35/35"
9. System automatically starts parsing:
   - "Parsing CV 1 of 35... 3% complete"
   - "Parsing CV 15 of 35... 43% complete"
   - "Parsing complete. 33 successful, 2 failed"
10. David reviews the 2 failed CVs:
    - One is an image-only PDF (needs OCR)
    - One is corrupted
11. Makes note to request better formats
12. Clicks "View Candidates" for the job
13. Sees 33 parsed candidate cards with:
    - Name, email, years of experience
    - Extracted skills (color-coded: match/partial/missing)
    - Education summary
14. Clicks "Run Ranking Algorithm"
15. System calculates match scores (takes 5 seconds)
16. Candidates re-sorted by match score (highest first)
17. Top candidate: 92% match - Michael Chen
    - Has: Python ✓, React ✓, AWS ✓, Docker ✓
    - Experience: 7 years ✓
18. David exports top 10 candidates to PDF
19. Shares with hiring manager via email

### Expected Outcome
✅ All 35 CVs uploaded in < 2 minutes  
✅ 33 successfully parsed (94% success rate)  
✅ Candidates ranked objectively  
✅ Top 10 shortlist delivered to hiring manager within 30 minutes  
✅ David saved 4+ hours compared to manual process  

### System Requirements
- Drag-and-drop file upload
- Batch processing
- Real-time progress indicators
- Error handling with clear messages
- Automatic parsing on upload
- One-click ranking
- PDF export functionality
- Mobile-responsive (David checks on phone later)

     3. AWS (32 candidates)
     4. React (30 candidates)
     5. Docker (25 candidates)

     - Screening: 45 candidates
     - Interview: 18 candidates
     - Offer: 5 candidates

## Scenario 4: Investigate Candidate Match Details (HR_RECRUITER)
**Persona:** David Levi - HR_RECRUITER  
**Goal:** Understand why candidate ranked #3 instead of #1  
**Frequency:** Daily, during candidate review  

### Context
David is reviewing candidates for "DevOps Engineer" position. The hiring manager questioned why Sarah Johnson is #3 when she seems highly qualified on paper.

### Steps
1. David opens "DevOps Engineer" job (JOB-2026-018)
2. Views ranked candidate list:
   - #1: Tom Williams - 94% match
   - #2: Lisa Chen - 91% match
   - #3: Sarah Johnson - 88% match
3. Clicks on Sarah Johnson's card to view details
4. System shows match breakdown:
   
   **Skills Match: 85%**
   - Required: Kubernetes ✓ (5 years)
   - Required: AWS ✓ (4 years)
   - Required: Python ✓ (6 years)
   - Required: Terraform ✗ (not found)
   - Preferred: Jenkins ✓ (3 years)
   
   **Experience Match: 90%**
   - Required: 5+ years ✓ (7 years total)
   - Relevant industry: FinTech ✓
   
   **Education Match: 100%**
   - B.S. Computer Science ✓
   
   **Overall: 88%**
   - Skills: 85% × 50% weight = 42.5%
   - Experience: 90% × 30% weight = 27%
   - Education: 100% × 20% weight = 20%
   - **Total: 89.5% → 88%**

5. David sees Sarah is missing Terraform experience
6. Clicks "View Full CV" to double-check
7. Reads through CV and confirms: no Terraform mentioned
8. Compares with #1 candidate (Tom):
   - Tom has all 4 required skills including Terraform
   - Tom has 8 years experience vs. Sarah's 7
9. David understands the ranking now
10. Adds note to Sarah's profile: "Strong candidate, missing Terraform but could learn quickly. Consider for interview."
11. Moves Sarah to "Interview Pool" despite being #3
12. Sends explanation to hiring manager:
    "Sarah ranks #3 due to missing Terraform skill, but her strong AWS and K8s experience makes her interview-worthy."

### Expected Outcome
✅ David understands exact reason for ranking  
✅ Can explain objective criteria to hiring manager  
✅ Makes informed decision to interview despite #3 ranking  
✅ System transparency builds trust  
✅ Hiring manager satisfied with explanation  

### System Requirements
- Detailed match breakdown
- Visual skill comparison (checkmarks, X marks)
- Weighted scoring explanation
- Link to original CV
- Note-taking capability
- Status update functionality
- Export explanation to share
